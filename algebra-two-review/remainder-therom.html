Remainder Therom

How do you write a Remainder?

Before we go into what the Remainder therom is, let's go over how to write an Remainder.

Let's say the quotient of a long divison of polynomials is: "p^2 + 5p + 6", the divisor is: "p-1", and the Remainder is 10

You will write the Remainder over/divided by the divisor next/ at the end of the quotient

in other words it'll look like this...

p^2 + 5p + 6 + 10 / p-1

What is ther Remainder therom?

It's basically means that and see and the Remainder of a long division problem without actually doing the long division. This also means we get to wither or not the divisor is a factor of the dividen

Now let's see this in action...

Is x-3 a factor of p(x) = x^2 - 11x + 24?

When a polynomial p(x) is divided by a binomial (x-a), then the remainder will always be p(a)

In english this means you take the opposite sign of the number in the divisior (so if number is -2, you change it + 2), then youplug in that number any where you see a variable in the eqquation

After you plug in numbers check to see if the number is zero

if it is zero the equation has no remainder and is in turn a factor. If it's not zero it has a remainder and is not a factor

Take the problem....

The number in that divisor is -3 so I'll change it to 3

Then I plug in 3 where ever I see a variable

(3)^2 - 11(3) + 24

Then I'll solve it

3^2 = 9

-11*3 = -33

9 - 33 = -24

-24 + 24 = 0

As you can see I got zero which means x-3 is a factor

Another Example

Is x-4 a factor of x^3 - 7x - 6?

The number in that divisor is -4 so I'll change it to 4

Then I plug in 4 where ever I see a variable

(4)^3 - 7(4) - 6

4^3 = 64

-7 * 4 = -28

64 - 28 = 36

36 - 6 = 30

As you can see I did not get zero which means x-4 is not a factor

side note: the remainder here is thirty and would be written like: 30 / x-4
